Automating Law Firms’ “Fund Review” Processes using AI Document Intelligence and Generative AI

React .NET Core SQL Server Azure AI Document Intelligence Generative AI

Thesis Summary

This master thesis explores the automation of the Fund Review process conducted by law firms for asset management companies. The goal is to replace repetitive, manual document evaluations with an intelligent automation framework combining AI Document Intelligence, Regex extraction, Knowledge graphs, and Generative AI.

Abstract

The thesis addresses the challenges of automating legal document processing, where the lack of labeled data and the domain specificity of legal language limit traditional AI models. To overcome these limitations, a hybrid AI architecture is introduced — combining a Knowledge Graph (KG) as a structured legal reasoning framework and a Large Language Model (LLM) as a natural language interface. Instead of relying on unverified LLM outputs, the KG externalizes all legal reasoning, ensuring verifiable, context-aware document interpretation.

Research Contributions

  • Knowledge Graph Construction: Legal documents are parsed into hierarchical chunks and transformed into semantic graph nodes, preserving cross-references and relationships.
  • Automated Taxonomy Discovery: A legal taxonomy emerges via graph-based community detection (Louvain algorithm), forming clusters of interconnected legal concepts.
  • Graph-RAG Framework: Integrates knowledge graph retrieval with LLM generation.

Methodology & Design

  • Custom document splitters parse constitutions, prospectuses, and agreements into semantically coherent sections using regex and embeddings.
  • Legal concepts are interlinked through semantic embeddings to capture both explicit and implicit references.
  • A Knowledge Graph represents entities and relations extracted from documents via LLM-assisted entity recognition and ontology alignment.
  • A community detection algorithm produces an emergent taxonomy of legal categories for fund review automation.
  • The GraphRAG system summarizes legal texts.

Technologies & Tools

  • Python.
  • Use of an OWL ontology file.
  • Graph database system: Neo4j
  • Cloud Infrastructure: Microsoft Azure for delivery.
  • AI Components: OpenAI GPT-5, text-embedding-3-small for the OpenAI embedder.

Expected Impact

  • Increased operational efficiency and reduced manual effort in fund review workflows.
  • Improved compliance accuracy and legal interpretability.
  • Potential extension toward fully autonomous legal and financial review systems.
