Project Description

Automating the "Fund Review" Process in Law Firms with AI and Hybrid Knowledge Graphs
This project is based on the thesis: "Automate Law Firms 'Fund Review' processes using AI Document Intelligence and Generative AI".

The "Fund Review" process for asset management companies requires legal experts to analyze investment funds across regulatory, legal, financial, and operational dimensions. Traditionally, this is a highly manual, repetitive, and error-prone task. This project introduces an AI-powered solution that automates the analysis and classification of legal documents—such as fund prospectuses, constitutions, and agreements—by combining Document Intelligence, regex-based parsing, decision trees, and Generative AI.

Unlike traditional supervised models—which struggle due to the lack of labeled legal data—this solution builds a hybrid architecture: a symbolic knowledge graph extracted from legal texts, paired with a Generative AI model used as a language interface (not as a source of truth). Legal reasoning and document structure are externalized and grounded in this knowledge graph, enabling legally valid and interpretable outputs.

  • Custom structural parsing (with regex and semantic analysis) segments legal documents into hierarchical, semantically-coherent chunks.
  • Entity and relationship extraction (using LLMs and ontology alignment) builds a knowledge graph capturing the legal content and cross-document references.
  • Community detection algorithms (e.g., Louvain method) induce a legal taxonomy—clusters of related clauses and concepts—directly from the graph without relying on predefined categories.
  • Generative AI provides natural language labels and enables context-aware search and summarization, always grounded in the underlying graph.

The result: a fully automated pipeline that transforms unstructured legal corpora into a navigable taxonomy, supporting attorneys in drafting fund review reports with improved efficiency, compliance, and reduced error. The framework is implemented in Python, with plans for React/.NET Core/Azure integration.

Skills & Technologies

  • Knowledge graph construction and ontology alignment
  • Regex-based and semantic parsing of legal documents
  • Community detection and unsupervised taxonomy extraction
  • Generative AI for language labeling and retrieval (Graph-RAG)
  • Python, Docker, and cloud deployment (Azure)
  • Legal tech domain expertise (fund prospectuses, compliance, legal classification)

Code Logic

  • Parse and segment documents into legal chunks using document-specific rules.
  • Extract entities and relationships with LLM-assisted functions; build a knowledge graph.
  • Run community detection to find clusters representing legal categories.
  • Label clusters with LLM and expose as a hierarchical taxonomy for attorneys.
  • Enable retrieval and drafting of legal text via Graph-augmented RAG.

Results & Impact

  • Automated creation of legal knowledge graphs and taxonomies from raw legal corpora.
  • Enabled rapid, accurate fund review drafting with less manual effort and improved compliance.
  • Demonstrated hybrid AI + symbolic reasoning for trustworthy, interpretable legal NLP.
