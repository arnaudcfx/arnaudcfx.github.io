This project implements adversarial search algorithms for Pacman vs. ghost gameplay, including Minimax, H-Minimax, and custom heuristics with cutoff strategies. The AI is designed to maximize Pacman's score while avoiding ghosts in a deterministic maze environment.

Problem Statement

  • Pacman: max player aiming to eat all food dots.
  • Ghost: min player trying to catch Pacman.
  • Food dots: passive entities that contribute to utility when eaten.
  • Utility function evaluates game outcomes with +500/-500 for wins/losses.

Implementation

  • Minimax handles game tree exploration with cycle detection to avoid infinite loops.
  • H-Minimax incorporates heuristics based on distance to food and ghosts.
  • Heuristic functions define Pacman strategy depending on safety and distance to remaining food.
  • Cutoff functions prevent unnecessary recursion when maximum depth or safe states are reached.

Experiments

Performance was evaluated by expanded nodes, execution time, and score for different ghost behaviors. H2 heuristic was the most efficient and unbeatable against smart and greedy ghosts.
